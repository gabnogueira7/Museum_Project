200 years of history

The origins of the Museum's rich and varied collection lie with the Society of Antiquaries of Scotland, which was founded in 1780, very much in the spirit of the Enlightenment, to collect the archaeology of Scotland. This collection passed into public ownership in 1851, forming the original collections of the National Museum of Antiquities of Scotland.
